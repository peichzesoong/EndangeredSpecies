Amur Leopard

100

Amur Leopards left in this world.

Conservation Status

Critically Endangered

The Amur Leopard (Panthera pardus orientalis) is a leopard subspecies native to the Primorye region of southeastern Russia and northern China. It is listed as Critically Endangered on the IUCN Red List.

The Amur leopard is solitary. Nimble-footed and strong, it carries and hides unfinished kills so that they are not taken by other predators.They live for 10-15 years, and in captivity up to 20 years.


Threats

Poaching

Poaching of leopards is a main threat to their survival. Leopards are most often killed by local Russians from small villages in and around the leopard habitat.

Forest Degradation

Human induced fires are a main threat to the survival of the leopard which cause degradation of suitable leopard habitat into unsuitable habitat.

Development Projects

The use of the road network established for the transport of logs from forests increases anthropogenic pressures in unprotected leopard habitat.

Inbreeding

The remaining population could disappear as a result of genetic degeneration, even without direct human influence.
